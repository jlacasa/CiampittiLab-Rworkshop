## 4                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     The soil of the experimental site is classified as Caplis very fine sandy loam (coarse-silty over clayey, mixed over smectitic, superactive, calcareous, thermic Oxyaquic Udifluvents) with an average slope of<1%. The split-plot, randomized complete block experiment was established in winter 2015. The study included three replicates of two tillage treatments, no-till (NT) (since 2014) and conventional till (CT), as main plots, with CC sub-plots of wheat (Triticum aestivum), hairy vetch (Vicia villosa) and control with native vegetation. Each sub-plot was 6.1 by 137 m. Progeny wheat (90 kg haâ\210’1 pure live seed; PLS) and BWI hairy vetch (30 kg haâ\210’1 PLS) were planted at a row spacing of 20 cm using a seed drill on Nov 14, Nov 15, and Oct 27 in 2015, 2016 and 2017, respectively. The CCs were not fertilized or irrigated, and weeds, pests or diseases were not controlled. Cover crops were terminated by spraying with paraquat on Mar18, Mar 31, and Mar 12 in 2016, 2017 and 2018, respectively. Soybean varieties NK-BY1212083, CZ4818 LL, and HBK 4953 L L were planted on a 1.02m row spacing at 296,526 seeds haâ\210’1 on Apr 25, Apr 28 and May 15 and harvested on Oct 10, Sept 27, and Oct 6 in 2016, 2017 and 2018, respectively. Each soybean plot was 6 rows wide by 137m long. Lower N accumulation under NT, especially in 2017, could be due to lower soil temperatures and the denser plow layer under surface residue, which may have led to decreased activity of rhizobium bacteria and constricted development of root nodules.  Thus, vetch potentially added 240 and 167 kg N haâ\210’1 to CT and NT soil, respectively, in 2016, but only 63.4 and 56.5 kg N haâ\210’1, respectively, in 2017. The CC biomass also accumulated higher amounts of P, S, and Fe under CT than NT.

Cover crops were terminated by spraying with paraquat on Mar18, Mar 31, and Mar 12 in 2016, 2017 and 2018, respectively. Soybean varieties NK-BY1212083, CZ4818 LL, and HBK 4953 L L were planted on a 1.02m row spacing at 296,526 seeds haâ\210’1 on Apr 25, Apr 28 and May 15 and harvested on Oct 10, Sept 27, and Oct 6 in 2016, 2017 and 2018, respectively.   • rename(): renames columns

  • group_by(): groups data based on levels of a variable. Subsequent functions will be treated for that group.
